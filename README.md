Thanh Tran 
Anne Pope 

## Project overview 
Can we mathematically define "morality" in fiction? We project 2,000 characters into an SVD-derived trait space [^1] and use Moral Foundations Theory (MFT) [^2] to identify characters who are aligned with each of the moral foundations.

## Motivation
Character analysis is traditionally subjective. As the story unfolds, we \textbf{feel} who the good guys are. As much as ambiguity gives life its depth, dilemmas can make narratives powerful. Nuanced characters often are often compelling due to their unclear motives and thus challenge readers to question their own moral assumptions. We are curious to learn about human complexity - our ability to consider nuances, our ability to predict consequences, our ability to choose one action over another. We attempt to use quantitative methodology to measure characters' morality.
